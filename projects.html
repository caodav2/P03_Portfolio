CLEAN TREETMENT

The topic we chose was microbeads being a pollutant to the Burrard Inlet. We chose this topic as a group because we felt so closely related to it, geographically and user-wise. The Burrard Inlet was literally a matter of a sky train away and as basic users of products that contain microbeads, such as face wash, we wanted to bring awareness and eliminate the use of microbeads for the better of the inlet and the marine animals that resides there.

CLEAN TREETMENT process

Choosing a topic: As a group we had to determine and choose a topic concerning threats to the environment. For the assigned task, we were to create a graphical information design, a short animation or a webpage regarding the chosen topic and spread the word about it, show solutions, and being informative to our audience.

INTERACTIVE ENVIRONMENT process

The second component of this project focused on creating a static environment that would be suitable and reasonable for the animal to reside within. For this component, I decided to create an artic-like environment for the penguin. The main purpose of this milestone was still dictated by the use and exploration of the geometric shapes.

The final milestone was the most challenging milestone because it required me to incorporate interaction from the animal and the environment. In order to do this, it required students to discover and learn the aspects of rotation, transformation and scaling.
