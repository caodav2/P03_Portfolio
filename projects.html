CLEAN TREETMENT

Clean Treetment is a project that my partner Giselle and I worked on for a design course. The objective of this project was to develop a company from the ground up. This included tasks such as, branding, logo, name, mission and goals of the company. Clean Treetment is a company that inspires to provide our customers with healthy homes. We are a organic and eco-friendly, cleaning company.

CLEAN TREETMENT process

Logo Variation: The most important aspect of our company was to determine and create a logo that represented our goals and values. We began ideating and moved from one brand logo to another, we filtered down to what we desired in our logo, simple and "clean".

Logo: As a group, we opted for the logo furthest to the right. This water droplet shaped like leave, was ideal to us, as it outline the importance of what we wanted to be represented as. A inner curvature leave to brush off the impression of "nature and natural" and an exterior water droplet that shows we are a cleaning company.

CLEAN TREETMENT challenges

Throughout the project, we faced many difficulties as this was our first ever design course. The most conflicting problem we had was deciding on how we, as a company, wanted to be seen. We wanted our logo to represent, nature, organic, tranquility and cleaniless. However, we didn't want to overcrowd our brand because we wanted to be recognizable immediately and iconic through a simply logo. The ideation process of designing a enticing logo truly took a signicant amount of time, as a result, we reduced our logo to two major components, a water droplet and a leaf.

INTERACTIVE ENVIRONMENT

This project began by building an animal from scratch using Processing. I opted to choose to replicate a cartoon penguin. The main purpose of this particular project was to explore and discover the use of basic geometric shapes within the processing environment which included, polygons, ellipse’s, rectangles, triangles and quads. The basic shapes such as ellipse and rectangle were easy to implement and understand. However, the more difficult shapes consisted of the triangle and quads.

INTERACTIVE ENVIRONENT challenges

This project is iconic and important to me because I felt that it is a huge accomplishment. Prior to this assignment, I had little to no experience in coding. The feeling of accomplishment was from the steep learning curve to the completed product. Along the way, it was difficult and challenging to learn new functions, code, and even to an extent that basics of using the program Processing to compile a functioning and aesthetically pleasing assignment.

The importance of this project is highlighted by the use of Processing. Processing became a enormous part of my university career. To be successful in my program (Interactive Arts and Technology), I had to be proficient with processing because, a lot of future courses required the minimum knowledge with this application. Processing and I have a love-hate relation but this project was certainly a stepping stone for myself in terms of learning how to use Processing.

INTERACTIVE ENVIRONMENT process

The second component of this project focused on creating a static environment that would be suitable and reasonable for the animal to reside within. For this component, I decided to create an artic-like environment for the penguin. The main purpose of this milestone was still dictated by the use and exploration of the geometric shapes.

The final milestone was the most challenging milestone because it required me to incorporate interaction from the animal and the environment. In order to do this, it required students to discover and learn the aspects of rotation, transformation and scaling.
